How to Use This Assessment

  1. Score Importance (I): How critical is this capability to your business goals? (1 = nice-to-have, 10 = business-critical)
  2. Score Readiness (R): How prepared is your organization to execute? (1 = major gaps, 10 = fully operational)
  3. Identify Gaps: High I + Low R = Urgent priorities for investment
  4. Validate Strengths: High I + High R = Competitive advantages to protect
  5. Deprioritize: Low I capabilities can be addressed later regardless of readiness

AI LENS Connecting This Assessment to the Agentic Placemats

Placemat 1: The Agentic O2C

Capabilities with High I + Low R in the Invoice, Collect, and Recognize phases are prime candidates for:

  • Developer Agents (NOW): Automate integration code for billing, rating, and revenue recognition
  • Diagnostic Agents (6-12M): Proactive monitoring for dunning, collections, and compliance
  • Orchestration Agents (12-24M): End-to-end remediation across Invoice → Collect → Recognize

Ask: "Which of these gaps could an agent fix without a human ticket?"

Placemat 2: The Unified Growth Engine

Capabilities with High I + Low R in the Quote & Sell, Learn, and Sustain & Grow phases reveal PLG/SLG bridge opportunities:

  • Local Developer Tools (NOW): Unified catalog and pricing backbone across channels
  • Remote Agent Server (6-12M): Same customer intelligence powering both PLG portals and SLG sales tools
  • Predictive Agents (12-24M): Churn detection blending CDP usage data with CRM contract data

Ask: "Does this gap exist because PLG and SLG use different systems?"
